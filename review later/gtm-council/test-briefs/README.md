# GTM Council Test Briefs

Use these briefs to verify that the strengthened council produces differentiated analysis, evidence-calibrated votes, preserved disagreement, and concrete validation steps.

## Test 1: Clearly Weak Idea

### Opportunity

An AI app that lets consumers generate personalized motivational wallpapers every morning.

### Target Customer

General smartphone users. Buyer and user are the same person.

### Problem

People want more personalized phone backgrounds and daily motivation.

### Current Alternatives

Free wallpaper apps, Pinterest, Canva, built-in phone wallpapers, and doing nothing.

### Proposed Solution

Mobile app that generates a new motivational wallpaper every day using a short mood prompt.

### Business Model

$9.99 per month subscription.

### Distribution Hypothesis

TikTok virality, app store search, and influencer posts.

### Evidence Already Available

Friends said the demo is fun. No paid users, no search data, no retention data, and no channel tests.

### Constraints

Solo founder, four-week build window, no marketing budget.

### Decision Needed

Should this be pursued beyond a weekend prototype?

### Expected Council Behavior

Agents should reject or express serious concern quickly, cite weak demand and distribution evidence, identify cheap alternatives, and avoid over-crediting demo appeal.

## Test 2: Promising But Under-Evidenced Idea

### Opportunity

A lightweight scheduling and follow-up tool for independent physical therapists who lose revenue when patients fail to book follow-up sessions.

### Target Customer

Independent physical therapy clinic owners. Users are front-desk staff and clinicians.

### Problem

Clinics rely on manual reminders and inconsistent follow-up after initial appointments.

### Current Alternatives

Practice management systems, spreadsheets, manual calls, SMS reminders, and no structured follow-up process.

### Proposed Solution

Simple follow-up workflow that identifies patients due for rebooking, drafts messages, and tracks whether they schedule.

### Business Model

$99 to $199 per clinic per month.

### Distribution Hypothesis

Founder-led outreach to local clinics, niche PT communities, and referrals from billing consultants.

### Evidence Already Available

Three clinic owners described the problem and one asked to test a prototype. No paid commitment, retention data, or integration proof.

### Constraints

Two-person team, eight-week MVP window, must avoid complex EHR integrations at first.

### Decision Needed

What must be validated before building a full MVP?

### Expected Council Behavior

Agents should avoid fake certainty, define the narrow ICP, expose integration and workflow risks, and produce targeted validation steps.

## Test 3: Complex B2B Idea

### Opportunity

Compliance evidence automation for mid-market fintech companies preparing for SOC 2 and bank partner reviews.

### Target Customer

VP Operations, Head of Compliance, and CTO at fintech companies with 50 to 500 employees. Users include compliance managers, engineering leads, and security teams.

### Problem

Teams spend weeks collecting evidence across systems for audits, vendor reviews, and bank partner diligence.

### Current Alternatives

Vanta, Drata, Secureframe, spreadsheets, consultants, internal scripts, and manual screenshots.

### Proposed Solution

Workflow that connects to operational systems, maps controls to evidence, flags stale artifacts, and prepares review-ready evidence packets.

### Business Model

Annual contracts from $18,000 to $60,000, depending on company size and systems connected.

### Distribution Hypothesis

Founder-led sales through fintech operator networks, compliance consultants, and content targeted at bank partnership readiness.

### Evidence Already Available

Five fintech operators confirmed evidence collection is painful. Two use Vanta but still rely on manual evidence packets. No signed pilot yet. Integrations and compliance liability are unresolved.

### Constraints

Small technical team, must handle sensitive data, likely long sales cycles, and high trust requirements.

### Decision Needed

Is there a credible wedge against existing compliance platforms, and what must be proven first?

### Expected Council Behavior

Agents should surface buyer/user mismatch, incumbent risk, sales cycle, integration burden, compliance/security requirements, pricing proof, and a concrete wedge validation plan.
