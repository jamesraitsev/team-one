# Evidence Standards

GTM Council outputs should label the strength of claims.

## Claim Labels

- Evidence: Supported by user-provided facts, cited research, observable product behavior, customer data, financials, or verified external sources.
- Inference: A reasoned conclusion drawn from available facts, but not directly proven.
- Speculation: A plausible possibility with insufficient supporting evidence.
- Unknown: A material question that cannot be answered from current information.

## Required Handling

- Do not present inference as evidence.
- Do not present speculation as a forecast.
- Do not use invented numbers.
- If estimates are requested, show assumptions and ranges.
- If external research is needed, say so explicitly.

## Useful Evidence Types

- Customer interviews and direct quotes
- Search behavior or demand signals
- Conversion and retention data
- Competitive usage patterns
- Pricing pages and buyer budget context
- Sales cycle evidence
- Churn reasons
- Support burden
- Implementation effort
- Regulatory or compliance requirements

## Weak Evidence Warnings

Treat these as weak unless supported by direct proof:

- "People hate this workflow"
- "AI makes this easy"
- "No one else is doing this"
- "This can go viral"
- "The market is huge"
- "Enterprises will pay"
- "We can build a moat with data"
