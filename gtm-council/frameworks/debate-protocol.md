# Debate Protocol

Use this protocol after specialists complete independent reviews.

## Goals

- Surface material disagreement.
- Challenge critical assumptions.
- Reduce duplicate reasoning.
- Preserve minority views.
- Convert unresolved issues into validation tasks.
- Focus debate on the top three decision-controlling assumptions.
- Separate evidence disputes from judgment disputes.

## Sequence

1. Independent specialist review
2. Director builds an assumption register from the specialist reviews
3. Director selects the top three decision-controlling assumptions
4. Director maps agreement, disagreement, and evidence gaps for those assumptions
5. Specialists challenge the strongest opposing view or the weakest evidence claim
6. Director records the disagreement ledger and validation tasks
7. Specialists revise only if another agent exposed a real flaw
8. Independent voting
9. Director synthesis

## Debate Rules

- Argue from evidence, inference, or clearly labeled speculation.
- Challenge assumptions directly.
- Do not repeat another agent unless adding a materially different implication.
- Do not compromise to appear balanced.
- Do not hide low-confidence conclusions.
- Do not debate every concern; prioritize assumptions that can change the verdict.
- State whether disagreement is about facts, interpretation, weighting, or risk tolerance.
- Convert unresolved disagreement into a validation task with a confirming and disconfirming signal.

## Assumption Selection

The Director should choose assumptions that meet at least two of these criteria:

- If false, the final recommendation would likely change.
- Multiple agents depend on the assumption.
- Current evidence is weak or contested.
- The assumption controls near-term execution risk.
- It can be tested before major capital, hiring, or build commitment.

## Director Prompts

Use these prompts to facilitate debate:

- "Which assumption, if false, most damages the opportunity?"
- "Where are two agents interpreting the same evidence differently?"
- "Which concern is material enough to change the verdict?"
- "What would need to be proven in the next 30 days?"
- "Which minority concern should remain visible even if most agents vote to pursue?"
- "Is this disagreement about evidence, interpretation, or risk tolerance?"
- "What is the fastest test that could change this agent's vote?"
